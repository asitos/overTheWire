1. awk (The Column Crusher)

When data is arranged in columns (separated by spaces or tabs), awk is your best friend. It treats each line as a record and each word as a field ($1, $2, $3).

    Extract a specific column:

    awk '{print $2}' file.txt

    (Prints only the second word of every line.)


    Search and extract:

    awk '/keyword/ {print $3}' file.txt

    (Finds the line with "keyword" and prints the 3rd word.)


    Print the last column (even if line lengths vary):

    awk '{print $NF}' file.txt
